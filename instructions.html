Ceteris Paribus

Instructions

Interactive fiction (IF) is a text-based story-telling medium where the author and the reader (and sometimes, though not here, a random number generator) share control over the story. Most IF takes the alternating form of a conversation: the author's program begins the plot, the reader gives some input on which direction to take next, the program carries the plot forward accordingly, and so on.

Ceteris Paribus is the story of a civilization inhabiting a planet and an adjacent moon, a story of its struggle to escape troublesome cyclic patterns in its history. It is told in brief, headline-like sentences and via a story diagram in the background. As a reader you can choose either to let events unfold (by clicking the link marked “click to continue”) or to interfere (by click on links in the sidebar, once they are available). Interference will add or remove a marker from the story diagram. Survival is unlikely on your first read through, but you can always restart (by clicking the link “Restart to try for a better outcome?” that appears after civilization's demise) or undo a choice (by clicking the undo button in the other sidebar). Neither will erase the parts of the diagram you've uncovered.

Good luck, hope you enjoy, and thanks for reading!

—Mæja
